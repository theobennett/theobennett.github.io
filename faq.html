Frequently Asked Questions

Straightforward answers to common questions about our practice, services, and how to get started.

Getting Started

Start by completing our consultation request form. Our care coordinator will reach out within one business day to discuss fit, answer questions, and schedule your initial evaluation if it's a good match. The first appointment is a comprehensive evaluation, typically 60–90 minutes.

Your initial evaluation is a comprehensive 60–90 minute conversation. We review your current concerns, personal and family history, prior treatments, lifestyle factors, and goals. It's collaborative, not interrogative. By the end, you'll have a clear picture of our assessment and a proposed care plan to discuss and refine together.

We maintain a limited panel to ensure every patient receives meaningful attention. Submit a consultation request and we'll confirm current availability. We maintain a short waitlist during high-demand periods and will let you know our realistic timeline.

Cost & Insurance

We are a private-pay practice and do not bill insurance directly. However, we provide detailed superbills — itemized receipts with the diagnostic and procedure codes your insurance needs. If you have PPO insurance with out-of-network benefits, you may be able to submit for partial reimbursement, often 50–80% of the allowable rate after your deductible is met.

We're happy to help you understand your out-of-network benefits before you schedule.

Fees vary by visit type and length. Our initial evaluation is a 60–90 minute comprehensive appointment. Follow-up visits range from brief medication checks to full therapy sessions. We share our full fee schedule during your intake call before you commit to anything.

Our concierge plan is for patients who want same-day access, after-hours availability, and direct clinical communication outside of scheduled appointments. It includes a 24/7 nurse triage line, direct physician support for urgent medication questions, and extended scheduling hours (6 AM–8 PM). It's a good fit for anyone managing an active condition who values knowing there's always someone to reach.

No. As a private-pay practice, your care is between you and your provider. We do not bill through employer-sponsored insurance, so no explanation of benefits is sent to your insurer or employer. All records are maintained under strict HIPAA protections and are never shared without your explicit written consent.

Treatment-resistant depression and complex mood disorders are a clinical specialty of ours. We approach these cases differently: a thorough re-evaluation of diagnosis, pharmacogenomic testing when appropriate, and consideration of interventional options including TMS (available 2026) and other evidence-based approaches.
