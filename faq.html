Pricing & Payment

What does a consultation cost?

Your first appointment is a comprehensive 60–90 minute evaluation. This includes a full psychiatric assessment, diagnostic discussion, and treatment planning. The cost is $600.

Follow-up appointments vary based on complexity and time:

  • Brief follow-up (medication refill, check-in): $150 (15–20 min)
  • Standard follow-up (medication adjustment + therapy): $350 (30 min)
  • Complex follow-up (diagnostic work, multiple issues): $450 (45 min)

Do you offer a sliding scale or reduced fees?

We're a private-pay practice, so we don't have a sliding scale. However, we work with you on payment and are happy to discuss options if cost is a barrier. Reach out and let's talk about what works for your situation.

Do you take insurance?

Short answer: We're private-pay, but we help you maximize your insurance benefits.

Here's how it works:

  • You pay us at the time of service. We provide you with an itemized receipt and a "superbill" (detailed claim form) that includes all the information your insurance needs.
  • You submit the superbill to your insurance. If you have out-of-network benefits (which most PPO plans do), your insurance reimburses you directly, typically 50–80% of the "Usual, Customary & Reasonable" (UCR) rate.
  • We handle the paperwork. Our staff can help answer questions about your benefits and walk you through the submission process—you don't have to do this alone.

Many of our patients find they pay less out-of-pocket with our private-pay model than they would with in-network copays and deductibles. It depends on your specific plan.

In rare cases, we may pursue a Single Case Agreement or out-of-network exemption with your insurance (especially for complex/treatment-resistant cases). Ask us if this might apply to you.

How do I know what my insurance will reimburse?

Great question. Contact your insurance company and ask:

  • "What are my out-of-network mental health benefits?"
  • "What's my deductible and out-of-pocket maximum?"
  • "What's the typical reimbursement rate for out-of-network psychiatry?" (usually listed as percentage, like 50%, 70%, or 80%)

Once you know your benefits, we can estimate how much you'll pay out-of-pocket. Our staff is also happy to walk you through this conversation.

Confidentiality & Privacy

Is my information completely confidential?

Yes, absolutely. Everything you tell us is protected by law (California Mental Health Confidentiality Law, HIPAA, and medical privilege). We do not share your information with anyone—not your employer, not your family, not your insurance company—without your written consent.

The only exceptions (required by law) are:

  • If we have serious reason to believe you're a danger to yourself or others (we'd talk to you first)
  • If there's abuse of a child or dependent elder (we're mandated reporters)
  • If we receive a court order

Your privacy is sacred. We treat it that way.

Will my employer find out I'm seeing a psychiatrist?

No, unless you tell them. Here's why:

  • You pay us directly, so there's no claim going through your employer's health plan.
  • We provide superbills for you to submit to insurance, not explanations of benefits that go to your employer.
  • Your medical records are completely separate from your employment records. Your employer has no access to them.

Bottom line: Your psychiatric care is private. What you do with that information is your choice.

What if I need to take FMLA or disability leave?

That's completely your decision. If you decide to pursue FMLA, short-term disability, or long-term disability, we can provide documentation to support your claim. But we only do this with your explicit request—we don't initiate it.

We understand these are sensitive decisions, and we're happy to discuss how to navigate them in a way that protects your privacy while getting you the support you need.

Treatment & Outcomes

How long until I feel better?

This depends on what's going on, but here's what we typically see:

  • Anxiety: 2–4 weeks of medication adjustment, often with noticeable improvement in sleep and worry within that timeframe.
  • Depression: 4–6 weeks to find the right medication; most patients see meaningful improvement by week 6–8.
  • ADHD: 1–2 weeks to feel the effect of medication; clarity on what works in 2–4 weeks.

We measure your progress every visit using validated symptom scales, so we know if something's working or if we need to adjust.

Important: Feeling better takes time. We're in this for the long haul with you, not just chasing quick fixes.

What if medication doesn't work for me?

We have a systematic approach to medication-resistant cases. If the first medication doesn't work, we:

  • Adjust the dose
  • Switch to a different class of medication
  • Add a complementary medication to boost effectiveness
  • Consider specialized assessments (like pharmacogenomic testing) to predict what might work better

You're not alone in this. We specialize in treatment-resistant cases. If standard approaches aren't working, we'll keep working until we find what does.

Do I have to take medication?

No. Medication is one tool in the toolbox. Some people benefit from medication. Some prefer therapy alone. Some benefit from a combination.

We'll discuss all your options and let you decide what feels right for you. Our job is to inform you; your job is to choose.

What conditions do you treat?

We have expertise in:

  • Depression (including treatment-resistant depression)
  • Anxiety disorders (GAD, panic, social anxiety)
  • ADHD (diagnosis and medication management)
  • Bipolar disorder
  • PTSD and trauma-related conditions
  • OCD
  • Sleep disorders
  • General adult psychiatry across the full spectrum

If you have a specific condition you're not sure about, reach out and ask. We can talk about whether Altos is a good fit.

How long is the wait for an appointment?

No waitlists. We aim to schedule new patients within 1–2 weeks. For urgent situations, we often have same-day or next-day availability.

If you're in a crisis, please call us immediately or go to your nearest emergency room.

Do you offer telehealth (video) appointments?

Yes. We offer both in-person and telehealth appointments. Many patients prefer telehealth for convenience, and it works great for ongoing medication management and therapy.

Your first evaluation can be either in-person or via video—whatever works best for you.

What happens if I need help after hours?

If you're having a mental health emergency (thoughts of harming yourself, severe panic, etc.), please call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room.

National crisis resources:

  • National Suicide & Crisis Lifeline: Call or text 988
  • Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741

For non-emergency after-hours issues, we provide resources and guidance in your intake materials. If you're a concierge member (coming soon), you'll have 24/7 clinician access.
