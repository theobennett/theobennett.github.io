Section 1

Website Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 1, 2025

Altos Psychiatry, Inc. ("Altos Psychiatry," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy of visitors to our website (altospsychiatry.com) and individuals who contact us through our scheduling forms.

Attorney Review Required

This is a template privacy policy provided for informational purposes. It must be reviewed and customized by a California-licensed attorney familiar with HIPAA, CMIA, and CCPA before you publish it. Do not publish this version as-is.

Website visitors: We may collect standard web analytics data (pages visited, browser type, approximate location) through third-party analytics tools. This data is aggregated and not linked to individual identities.

Scheduling form submissions: When you submit a consultation request, we collect your name, contact information, and general description of the reason for reaching out. This information is used solely for scheduling and practice management purposes.

What we do not collect through this website: We do not collect protected health information (PHI) through our public website. All clinical intake is conducted through our HIPAA-compliant patient management platform (SimplePractice).

  • To respond to your consultation request or inquiry
  • To coordinate your care and scheduling
  • To improve our website and services
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations

We use the following third-party services that may process data on our behalf:

  • SimplePractice — HIPAA-compliant patient management, scheduling, and telehealth. Governed by a signed Business Associate Agreement (BAA).
  • Google Workspace — Email communications. Governed by a signed BAA for HIPAA-covered communications.
  • Google Analytics — Website analytics (non-clinical, aggregate data only).

Retention: Scheduling inquiry data submitted through our website is retained for 12 months and then deleted. Clinical records are maintained in accordance with California law (minimum 10 years for adult patients).

Your rights (California residents): Under the CCPA and CMIA, you have the right to request access to, correction of, or deletion of personal information we hold. Contact privacy@altospsychiatry.com to exercise these rights.

We do not sell your personal information to third parties.
