Website Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 1, 2025

Altos Psychiatry, Inc. ("Altos Psychiatry," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy of visitors to our website (altospsychiatry.com) and individuals who contact us through our scheduling forms.

Attorney review required. This is a template privacy policy provided for informational purposes. It must be reviewed and customized by a California-licensed attorney familiar with HIPAA, CMIA (California Medical Information Act), and CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act) before you publish it. Information We Collect

Website visitors: We may collect standard web analytics data (pages visited, browser type, approximate location) through third-party analytics tools. This data is aggregated and not linked to individual identities.

Scheduling form submissions: When you submit a consultation request, we collect your name, contact information, and general description of the reason for reaching out. This information is used solely for scheduling and practice management purposes. It is stored in a password-protected spreadsheet system and is not shared with third parties except as required by law.

What we do not collect through this website: We do not collect protected health information (PHI) through our public website. All clinical intake is conducted through our HIPAA-compliant patient management platform (SimplePractice).

How We Use Your Information

  • To respond to your consultation request or inquiry
  • To coordinate your care and scheduling
  • To improve our website and services
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations

Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services that may process data on our behalf:

  • SimplePractice — HIPAA-compliant patient management, scheduling, and telehealth. Governed by a signed Business Associate Agreement (BAA).
  • Google Workspace — Email communications. Governed by a signed BAA for HIPAA-covered communications.
  • Google Analytics — Website analytics (non-clinical, aggregate data only).

Data Retention

Scheduling inquiry data submitted through our website is retained for 12 months and then deleted. Clinical records are maintained in accordance with California law (minimum 10 years for adult patients).

Your Rights (California Residents)

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Medical Information Act (CMIA), California residents have the right to request access to, correction of, or deletion of personal information we hold. To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@altospsychiatry.com.

We do not sell your personal information to third parties.

Our Duties

Altos Psychiatry, Inc. is required by law to maintain the privacy of your protected health information (PHI), to provide you with this notice of our legal duties and privacy practices, and to follow the terms of the notice currently in effect.

How We May Use and Disclose Your Health Information

Treatment: We may use and share your PHI to provide, coordinate, or manage your healthcare and related services. For example, we may share information with other providers involved in your care, with your written consent.

Payment: We may use and share your PHI to bill and receive payment for services we provide. For example, we may provide your PHI to your insurance company to obtain reimbursement for services provided.

Healthcare operations: We may use and share your PHI to operate our practice, improve the quality of care, and conduct other business activities permitted or required by law.

Required by law: We may disclose PHI when required by federal, state, or local law, or in response to a valid court order or subpoena.

Public health & safety: We may disclose PHI to prevent or lessen a serious threat to health or safety, consistent with applicable law.

Your Rights Regarding Your Health Information

  • Right to inspect and copy your PHI
  • Right to request an amendment to your PHI
  • Right to an accounting of disclosures
  • Right to request restrictions on uses and disclosures
  • Right to request confidential communications
  • Right to a paper copy of this notice
