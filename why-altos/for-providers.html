The Referral Experience

What you can expect from us

We know your time is limited. Here's exactly how we handle referrals.

1-Day Response

Every referral gets a response within one business day. We contact you to confirm receipt, then reach out to the patient to schedule.

Closed-Loop Reports

After evaluation, you receive a clinical summary — diagnosis, treatment plan, and recommendations. Ongoing updates as clinically relevant changes occur.

Direct Access

You can reach the treating psychiatrist directly for clinical questions — not through three layers of scheduling staff. Provider-to-provider communication is how care should work.

Payment note for your patients: Altos Psychiatry is a private-pay practice. Patients pay at time of service and receive superbills for PPO out-of-network reimbursement. We do not participate in Medi-Cal or HMO panels. Most Bay Area PPO plans reimburse 50–80% of the allowable rate. For patients where cost is a barrier, please reach out and we'll discuss options.

How We Work Together

Built for providers who value coordination

After every evaluation, you receive a clinical summary including our diagnostic impression, treatment plan, and specific recommendations. If your patient starts treatment with us, you'll receive updates when clinically significant changes occur — medication adjustments, diagnostic revisions, or treatment milestones. No referral goes into a black hole.

You can reach the treating psychiatrist directly for clinical questions — not through three layers of scheduling staff. Whether it's a quick question about a medication interaction or a complex case discussion, provider-to-provider communication is how we believe care should work. We pick up the phone.

If you're the patient's therapist, we coordinate medication decisions around your therapeutic work — not in parallel. If you're their PCP, we flag psychiatric medications with metabolic implications and communicate proactively about labs and monitoring. We view you as part of the treatment team, not a peripheral stakeholder.

Beyond standard medication management and psychotherapy, we offer pharmacogenomic testing, metabolic monitoring (board-certified obesity medicine), Spravato/ketamine for treatment-resistant depression, and TMS therapy (launching 2026). If your patient has been through multiple failed trials or has a complex presentation, we have the tools and training to take a different approach.
