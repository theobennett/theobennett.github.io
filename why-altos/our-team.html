Founding Provider

Theo Stoddard-Bennett, MD

Founder & Psychiatrist · Stanford Chief Resident

Theo grew up in Montana, where he saw firsthand how gaps in mental health infrastructure shape lives — treatable conditions going unrecognized, families navigating crises with almost no professional support. That early experience planted a conviction that stayed with him through every stage of training: the quality of psychiatric care a person receives shouldn't depend on circumstance.

He earned his medical degree at UCLA as a David Geffen Scholar, receiving the Louis Jolyon West Award for Excellence in Psychiatry — the department's highest honor for a graduating student. He is currently completing psychiatry residency at Stanford, where he serves as Chief Resident overseeing inpatient, consultation-liaison, and emergency psychiatry services.

What sets Theo apart is the unusual breadth of his training. Across Stanford, Harvard, UCLA, and the VA system, he has studied under world experts spanning nearly every psychiatric subspecialty — from interventional treatments like TMS and ketamine-assisted therapy, to psychopharmacology, neuropsychiatry, child and adolescent mental health, forensic psychiatry, and consultation-liaison care. That cross-disciplinary foundation means he doesn't approach your care from a single lens.

Research & Scholarship

Theo's research spans over a decade and more than a dozen peer-reviewed publications — from neuroinflammation and suicide biology at UCLA, to TMS outcomes at Stanford, to machine learning models expanding rural psychiatric access in Montana. His work has been presented at international conferences, with a closing keynote address on personalized neurotherapeutics.

Earlier in his career, he spent four years in stem cell neuroscience, contributing to foundational research on dopaminergic neuron differentiation for Parkinson's disease — work published in Nature Communications, Scientific Reports, and Neural Regeneration Research.

He also served as Operations Director for the Los Angeles Human Rights Initiative, leading psychiatric evaluations and medical-legal documentation for asylum seekers.

Advocacy & Beyond the Clinic

Mental health advocacy has been central to Theo's life since long before medical school. He served as National President of NAMI, delivered keynote addresses at the national convention and state conferences, gave a TEDx talk on humanizing mental illness, and spent years as a court-appointed advocate for children in foster care.

He was named a Barry M. Goldwater Scholar by the United States Senate, a Knight-Hennessy Scholar finalist at Stanford, and a recipient of the President's Service Award.

Outside of medicine, Theo is happiest with his family and in the mountains. He's an avid skier, trail runner, and rock climber — drawn to the kind of quiet focus that comes from being in nature with people he loves. That same disposition shapes how he practices: present, unhurried, and genuinely invested in the person sitting across from him.

Our Standards

What every team member shares

Clinical Rigor

Every provider trains at respected academic medical centers and stays current with the evidence. We are willing to say when we don't know — and to refer when someone else can serve you better.

Genuine Availability

We cap panel sizes deliberately. A provider who is stretched too thin cannot give patients the attention they deserve — and will burn out. We treat provider wellbeing as a clinical quality issue.

Long-Term Thinking

Our goal is not to manage your condition indefinitely. It is to help you achieve durable remission — and to equip you with an understanding of your own neurobiology to sustain it.
