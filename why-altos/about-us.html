Where It Started

From Montana to the Peninsula

Before Stanford, before Menlo Park, there was Montana — a state with vast landscapes, tight-knit communities, and some of the most significant gaps in mental health care in the country.

Growing up there, you see firsthand what happens when people don't have access to thoughtful psychiatric care. You watch families navigate crises with almost no professional support. You see how treatable conditions go undiagnosed or undertreated — not because people don't want help, but because the infrastructure simply isn't there. And you see how those gaps compound over years, shaping outcomes in ways that didn't have to happen.

That experience left a mark. It shaped a conviction that quality of care shouldn't depend on where you happen to live — and that when you do have the opportunity to build something, you should build it right.

The Gap We Saw

Even in the Bay Area, the model is broken

Training at Stanford and leading academic medical centers provided access to the best psychiatric care in the world — cutting-edge research, multidisciplinary teams, the full weight of a world-class institution behind every patient.

But once patients leave that setting, the standard outpatient model looks very different. Appointments every four to six weeks. Fifteen minutes to cover medication, side effects, life changes, and whatever you've been carrying since your last visit. Voicemail during a crisis. A provider who sees so many patients they can't hold the nuance of your situation.

It's not that these providers don't care. The system they work within simply doesn't give them the room to practice the way they were trained to. The care you receive at a major academic center shouldn't evaporate the moment you step into a community setting.

Our Founding Vision

Academic rigor, community warmth

Altos Psychiatry was founded to bridge that gap — to deliver the same caliber of care you'd receive at a major academic center, in the privacy and comfort of a community practice that actually knows you.

We deliberately keep a smaller patient panel. Not because we want to be exclusive, but because psychiatric care done well demands time, continuity, and attention to detail. Every individual and every family deserves a provider who has the space to think carefully about their treatment — not one who's stretched across hundreds of patients and running behind before the day even starts.

This is the practice we wished existed when we saw what gaps in care can do. We built it to meet people where they are.

Our Model

What this looks like in practice

A smaller panel isn't a philosophy — it's a structure that makes better care possible.

Longer appointments

Your first visit is 60–90 minutes. Follow-ups are 30–45. Enough time to actually understand what's happening — and to make thoughtful decisions together.

Reachable between visits

Secure messaging for medication questions, emerging concerns, or side effects. You shouldn't have to wait weeks for answers to time-sensitive questions.

A provider who knows you

A smaller panel means your psychiatrist remembers your history, understands your goals, and notices when something shifts — without you having to re-explain every visit.

Integrated, not fragmented

Medication, therapy, and lifestyle factors coordinated by providers who communicate with each other — within one practice that holds your full clinical picture.

Core Values

What drives every decision we make

Availability

Mental health crises don't respect business hours. We build systems that let us be reachable for the moments that matter — without burning out our providers.

Whole-Person Care

Sleep, nutrition, metabolic health, and exercise are not peripheral to psychiatry. They are core clinical variables, and we treat them as such.

Continuity

Your psychiatric history is yours. If you move, need a hand-off, or seek a second opinion, we facilitate seamless transitions — never blank slates.

Clinical Rigor

Innovation without evidence is not care. We adopt new treatments when the data supports them, and we track outcomes to know whether they're working.
