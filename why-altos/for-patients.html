We know that reaching out to a psychiatrist — whether for yourself or someone you love — takes courage. Maybe you've been thinking about it for weeks. Maybe years. Maybe someone you trust finally said, "You deserve help with this."

Whatever brought you here, you're welcome. We don't rush. We don't judge. And we don't treat you like a chart number. From your very first conversation with us, we want you to feel like you're in the right place — like you've found people who genuinely care about what you're going through and have the expertise to help.

This page walks you through everything: what happens before, during, and after your first visit, how payment works, what our care plans look like, and how to reach us when you need to. No surprises. No fine print buried at the bottom.

The First Appointment

What to expect

Your initial evaluation is a 60–90 minute conversation — not a questionnaire, not a checklist, not a rushed 15-minute slot where you barely finish a sentence. We want to understand your full picture: what you're experiencing now, your history, your goals, what has and hasn't worked before, and — just as importantly — what matters to you in your life beyond your symptoms.

Think of it less like a doctor's appointment and more like sitting down with someone who's genuinely curious about your story and has the training to know what to do with it.

By the end of that first session, you'll have our clinical assessment and a proposed treatment plan to discuss together. Nothing is decided without your input. Nothing starts until you're comfortable.

What to bring

  • A list of current medications and dosages
  • Names of prior providers if you'd like records transferred
  • Notes on what's helped and what hasn't — even informally
  • Any questions you'd like to ask us (seriously — write them down)
  • A family member or partner, if you'd like them there for support

Before your visit: Intake forms will be sent through our secure patient portal (SimplePractice). Please complete them at least 24 hours ahead if possible — it lets us spend more of our time together on what matters instead of paperwork.

After Your First Visit

You're not on your own after that first conversation

Here's what happens next — so you always know where you stand.

Within 24 Hours

You'll receive a summary of our initial assessment and the proposed treatment plan through your patient portal. If we've ordered lab work or pharmacogenomic testing, you'll get clear instructions on next steps. If we started a medication, you'll know exactly what to watch for and when to reach out.

Your Next Appointment

Follow-ups are typically scheduled 2–4 weeks after your initial evaluation — closer together during the early phase when we're adjusting treatment, and gradually spaced out as you stabilize. We don't make you wait 6 weeks to find out if something is working.

Between Visits

You can always reach us. Our patient portal has secure messaging for non-urgent questions. If something comes up that can't wait — a side effect, a bad day, a question about your medication — we're responsive. You shouldn't have to white-knuckle it until your next appointment.

Ongoing Relationship

This isn't transactional care. Over time, we get to know you — your patterns, your triggers, what "baseline" looks and feels like for you. That depth of relationship is what allows us to notice when something shifts before it becomes a crisis. It's also what makes the work feel human.

Fees & Insurance

Straightforward pricing, no surprises

We are a private-pay practice, which means you pay at the time of service. We know that's a meaningful financial commitment, and we take it seriously — which is why we're transparent about costs upfront, before you ever walk through our door.

We provide detailed superbills — receipts formatted for insurance reimbursement — for patients who wish to seek reimbursement from their PPO insurance carrier.

Out-of-network reimbursement: If you have PPO insurance (common with Bay Area tech employers), you likely have out-of-network benefits that cover 50–80% of the allowable rate after your deductible is met. We recommend calling the member services number on the back of your insurance card and asking: "What are my out-of-network benefits for outpatient mental health?" We're happy to help you understand the answer.

Care Plans

Two levels of care — both built around you

Whether you choose our Standard or Concierge plan, you get the same clinical quality, the same attention, and the same team. The difference is in how much access and availability you want between visits.

Standard Plan

Core psychiatric care

Everything you need for high-quality, consistent treatment — without the extras. This is excellent care by any standard.

  • Medication management within scheduled appointments
  • Psychotherapy (individual, standalone or combined)
  • Telehealth and in-person options
  • Refill requests processed within 1 business day
  • Secure patient portal messaging
  • Superbills provided for insurance reimbursement

For patients who want reliable, high-quality care with defined touchpoints.

Concierge Plan

Elevated access & availability

Everything in Standard — plus the peace of mind that comes from knowing someone is always reachable when you need them.

  • Everything in the Standard Plan
  • 24/7 nurse triage line
  • After-hours MD support for urgent clinical needs
  • Extended scheduling hours (6 AM – 8 PM)
  • Patient-preferred communication (text, phone, or portal)
  • Same-day appointments (Premium add-on)
  • Priority scheduling for routine visits

For patients managing active conditions, going through a tough stretch, or who simply value knowing there's always someone to reach.

Concierge plan pricing is discussed during your intake call. Plans are billed monthly and can be cancelled with 30 days' notice. No long-term contracts.

For Families

If you're here for someone you love

We see you, too. Watching someone you care about struggle with their mental health is its own kind of pain — and it often comes with questions you don't know how to ask, decisions you're not sure you should make, and a helplessness that can be overwhelming.

Here's what we want you to know:

  • You're doing the right thing by looking into care. Even if the person you love isn't ready yet, the fact that you're researching options means you're already helping. Sometimes the most important thing a family member does is find the right door — so it's open when they're ready to walk through it.
  • You're welcome in the process. With the patient's permission, family members can participate in appointments, ask questions, and be part of treatment planning. Many of our patients find that having a trusted person involved — even just for the first visit — makes the experience less intimidating and more productive.
  • We'll communicate clearly with you. Within the boundaries of patient confidentiality, we make every effort to keep involved family members informed and supported. If you have concerns or observations between visits, we want to hear them — they're often clinically valuable.
  • Your wellbeing matters, too. Caring for someone with a mental health condition can take a real toll. If you're struggling, we're happy to provide referrals for your own support — whether that's individual therapy, a family therapist, or a support group. You can't pour from an empty cup.
  • There's no judgment here — for anyone. We know that families navigate mental health with their own histories, fears, and misconceptions. We meet everyone where they are, with patience and without judgment. That applies to you as much as it applies to the person seeking care.

Not sure how to start the conversation?

If you're trying to help a family member consider psychiatric care and aren't sure how to bring it up, we're happy to talk that through with you. Call or email us — no appointment needed, no obligation. Sometimes it helps just to hear, "Here's what other families have found works."
