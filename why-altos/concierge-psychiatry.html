How We Think About Care

Care Philosophy

Precision, availability, and the whole person — three principles that guide every clinical decision we make.

Standard psychiatric care often operates in a narrow lane: diagnose, prescribe, repeat. We take a broader view. Psychiatric conditions don't arise in isolation — they are shaped by sleep, nutrition, metabolic health, stress, social context, and neurobiology. Treating them effectively requires attending to all of those variables — and being available when it matters most.

Integrative Psychiatry

The whole person, not just the symptom

This is not alternative medicine. It is evidence-based psychiatry that takes seriously what the research increasingly confirms: that inflammatory markers, metabolic dysfunction, sleep dysregulation, and nutritional deficiencies are clinically relevant to mood, cognition, and treatment response.

Metabolic Psychiatry

We routinely assess and manage the metabolic side effects of psychiatric medications — weight gain, insulin resistance, dyslipidemia — that drive non-adherence. Our founder has advanced training in obesity medicine.

Pharmacogenomics

Where appropriate, we use genetic testing to predict how you metabolize medications — reducing trial-and-error and identifying drugs likely to be effective or poorly tolerated before you try them.

Lifestyle Medicine

Sleep, exercise, and nutritional optimization are prescribed as seriously as medication when the evidence supports it. These are clinical interventions, not suggestions.

The Concierge Model

Available when it matters — without burning out the clinician

The traditional psychiatric model has a structural problem: clinicians see too many patients to be genuinely available, so they see patients on rigid schedules and hope nothing acute happens in between. This works until it doesn't.

Our concierge model resolves this by deliberately limiting panel size and layering in systems that extend availability — a nurse triage line, secure messaging, after-hours support — without placing unsustainable demands on any individual provider. The result is a practice where crises get clinical attention, not voicemail.

Data-Informed Care

Measuring what matters

Psychiatric care is often evaluated subjectively — "how do you feel?" We supplement clinical judgment with validated outcome measures collected at every visit. This creates an objective longitudinal record of your progress, makes medication decisions more precise, and ensures we're not just managing — we're improving.

Outcome data also informs our practice. When we see patterns across patients, it drives clinical learning. We consider this a core part of responsible care.

Collaborative Partnership

Your input shapes your care

We don't hand down a treatment plan — we build one together. You understand your life, your priorities, your tolerance for side effects, and what "better" actually means to you. We bring clinical expertise. The best outcome emerges from combining both.

This includes being honest when something isn't working. If a medication isn't delivering results or a therapy approach isn't clicking, we name it directly and explore alternatives. We would rather have that conversation than let an ineffective plan drift forward.

Before starting or changing any medication, we explain the rationale, expected benefits, risks, alternatives, and what "success" looks like for this specific decision. You have full information and full voice. Nothing happens to your care without your understanding and agreement.

Psychiatry involves uncertainty — about diagnosis, about treatment response, about timelines. We don't paper over that with false confidence. We tell you what we know, what we don't know, and what we're watching for. Honest communication builds trust, and trust is the foundation of effective treatment.

We invest time in helping you understand your condition, your medications, and the logic behind your treatment plan — not because it's polite, but because research shows that informed patients have better outcomes, better adherence, and better therapeutic alliances. Education isn't an add-on. It's part of the treatment.

With your permission, we communicate directly with your therapist, primary care physician, and any other specialists involved in your care. No one operates in a vacuum. Aligned providers make better decisions — and your treatment plan stays coherent across every touchpoint.
