Getting Started

Becoming a patient

Start by completing our consultation request form. Our care coordinator will reach out within one business day to discuss fit, answer questions, and schedule your initial evaluation if it's a good match.

Your initial evaluation is a comprehensive 60–90 minute conversation. We review your current concerns, personal and family history, prior treatments, lifestyle factors, and goals. It's collaborative, not interrogative. By the end, you'll have a clear picture of our assessment and a proposed care plan to discuss and refine together.

Yes. We offer both telehealth (secure video) and in-person appointments at our Menlo Park office. Many patients use a mix of both depending on their schedule. Some services, such as TMS therapy, require in-person attendance.

Cost & Insurance

Fees, reimbursement, and confidentiality

We are a private-pay practice and do not bill insurance directly. However, we provide detailed superbills — itemized receipts with the diagnostic and procedure codes your insurance needs. If you have PPO insurance with out-of-network benefits, you may be able to submit for partial reimbursement, often 50–80% of the allowable rate after your deductible is met.

We're happy to help you understand your out-of-network benefits before you schedule.

Fees vary by visit type and length. Your initial evaluation is a 60–90 minute comprehensive appointment. Follow-up visits range from brief medication checks to full therapy sessions. We share our complete fee schedule during your intake call — before you commit to anything.

Our concierge plan is for patients who want same-day access, after-hours availability, and direct clinical communication outside of scheduled appointments. It includes a triage line, direct physician support for urgent medication questions, and extended scheduling hours (6 AM–8 PM). It's a good fit for anyone managing an active condition who values knowing there's always someone to reach.

Insurance-based practices require high patient volumes to be financially viable. That means shorter appointments, larger panels, and less availability between visits. By operating as a private-pay practice, we're able to offer longer visits, smaller caseloads, and the kind of responsive, personalized care that insurance-driven models structurally cannot support.

No. As a private-pay practice, your care is between you and your provider. We do not bill through employer-sponsored insurance, so no explanation of benefits is sent to your insurer or employer. All records are maintained under strict HIPAA protections and are never shared without your explicit written consent.

Care & Treatment

What we do and how we do it

We do both, and often together. Our psychiatrists are medical doctors who can prescribe and manage psychiatric medications. We also offer individual psychotherapy. Many patients benefit from integrated care — medication plus therapy — and we're well-positioned to coordinate that within a single practice.

It means we treat the whole person. We routinely assess sleep quality, diet, exercise, metabolic health, and stress patterns as part of your psychiatric evaluation — not as a peripheral concern, but as clinical variables that directly affect treatment outcomes. This informs how we approach medication choices, lifestyle recommendations, and the overall trajectory of your care.

Treatment-resistant conditions are a clinical focus of ours. We approach these cases differently: a thorough re-evaluation of diagnosis, pharmacogenomic testing when appropriate, and consideration of interventional options including TMS (available 2026) and other evidence-based approaches. If prior treatments haven't worked, that history is data — and it guides the next step, not a reason to start over from scratch.

Absolutely. We work closely with external therapists and actively support coordinated care. If you have a therapist you trust, we're happy to serve as the medication management partner and communicate with them as needed (with your consent). Continuity between your providers leads to better outcomes, and we take that coordination seriously.

We treat a wide range of adult psychiatric conditions including depression, anxiety disorders, ADHD, bipolar disorder, OCD, PTSD, insomnia, and complex presentations that haven't responded to standard treatment. Visit our What We Treat page for a full list.

Three things set us apart: time (longer appointments, smaller panels, genuine availability between visits), integration (medication, therapy, lifestyle, and future interventional modalities coordinated within one practice), and precision (pharmacogenomics, outcome tracking, and data-driven treatment adjustments). We built this practice to deliver the depth of care you'd expect at a major academic center — in the privacy and comfort of a community setting.

Scheduling & Logistics

Appointments, cancellations, and access

Current patients schedule through the patient portal (SimplePractice). You can also call our office at (650) 555-0000 during business hours. For new patients, the process starts with a consultation request.

We ask for 48 hours' notice for cancellations or rescheduling. Late cancellations and no-shows are subject to a fee. We understand that life happens — if something comes up, let us know as early as possible and we'll do our best to accommodate.

Through the secure patient portal. For non-urgent medication questions, side effect concerns, or brief updates, messaging is the fastest route. Concierge patients have additional access including extended-hours phone availability and direct physician messaging.

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, call 911. For crisis support, contact the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline at 988 (call or text, 24/7). The Crisis Text Line is also available — text HOME to 741741. Our practice is not an emergency service and cannot guarantee immediate response outside of business hours.
