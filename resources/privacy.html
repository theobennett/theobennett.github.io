HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices

Effective Date: [DATE] · Last Revised: [DATE]

THIS NOTICE DESCRIBES HOW MEDICAL INFORMATION ABOUT YOU MAY BE USED AND DISCLOSED AND HOW YOU CAN GET ACCESS TO THIS INFORMATION. PLEASE REVIEW IT CAREFULLY.

Our Duties

Altos Psychiatry, Inc. ("Practice") is required by law to maintain the privacy of your protected health information (PHI) and to provide you with notice of our legal duties and privacy practices. We are required to abide by the terms of this notice while it is in effect.

How We May Use and Disclose Your Health Information

Treatment. We may use and disclose your PHI for your treatment and to provide you with treatment-related health care services. For example, we may disclose your PHI to physicians, nurses, or other health care professionals who provide you with health care services.

Payment. We may use and disclose your PHI so that we or others may bill and receive payment from you, an insurance company, or a third party for the treatment and services you received. For example, we may disclose your PHI to obtain prior authorization from your insurance company.

Health Care Operations. We may use and disclose your PHI for our health care operations. Health care operations include the business aspects of running the Practice, such as quality assessments, training, and administrative activities.

Your Rights

You have the following rights regarding your PHI:

  • Right to Access. You may request copies of your health information.
  • Right to Amend. You may request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Right to an Accounting of Disclosures. You may request a list of certain disclosures we made of your PHI.
  • Right to Request Restrictions. You may ask us to restrict certain uses and disclosures.
  • Right to Confidential Communications. You may ask us to contact you in a specific way.
  • Right to a Paper Copy. You may request a paper copy of this notice at any time.

Complaints

If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with our Practice or with the Secretary of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. To file a complaint with our Practice, contact us at: privacy@altospsychiatry.com. We will not retaliate against you for filing a complaint.

Website Privacy Policy

Effective Date: [DATE]

This Privacy Policy describes how Altos Psychiatry, Inc. collects, uses, and shares information through the website altospsychiatry.com (the "Site").

Information We Collect

Information you provide. When you submit a consultation request or referral form on our Site, we collect contact information (name, email, phone) and the information you provide in those forms. These forms are for scheduling purposes only and are not HIPAA-covered.

Automatically collected information. We may collect standard web analytics data such as IP address, browser type, and pages visited. We do not sell this data.

How We Use Information

We use information submitted through this Site solely to follow up with prospective patients and referring providers regarding scheduling and fit. We do not sell, rent, or trade your information with third parties for marketing purposes.

Clinical Information

Your clinical health information — collected after you become a patient — is governed by our HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices above, not by this policy. Clinical information is handled exclusively through HIPAA-compliant systems (SimplePractice) and is never collected through this website.
