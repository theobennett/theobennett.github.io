I. HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices

Effective Date: July 1, 2025 · Last Revised: July 1, 2025

THIS NOTICE DESCRIBES HOW MEDICAL INFORMATION ABOUT YOU MAY BE USED AND DISCLOSED AND HOW YOU CAN GET ACCESS TO THIS INFORMATION. PLEASE REVIEW IT CAREFULLY.

A. Our Legal Obligations

Altos Psychiatry, Inc. ("Practice," "we," "us," or "our") is required by the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 ("HIPAA"), the HITECH Act, and their implementing regulations (45 CFR Parts 160 and 164) to:

  • Maintain the privacy of your protected health information ("PHI");
  • Provide you with this Notice of our legal duties and privacy practices with respect to your PHI;
  • Abide by the terms of this Notice currently in effect;
  • Notify you in the event of a breach of your unsecured PHI.

We reserve the right to change the terms of this Notice at any time. Any revised Notice will be effective for all PHI we maintain at that time. A copy of the current Notice will be available at our office and on our website.

B. How We May Use and Disclose Your PHI

1. Treatment

We may use and disclose your PHI to provide, coordinate, or manage your psychiatric care and any related services. This includes sharing information with other health care providers involved in your care — such as your primary care physician, therapist, or specialist — for the purpose of treatment coordination. For example, if you are receiving psychotherapy from one clinician and medication management from another within our practice, your treatment team will share relevant clinical information to provide integrated care.

2. Payment

We may use and disclose your PHI to obtain payment for services we provide. This may include providing information to your health insurance company to determine eligibility, obtain prior authorization, or process claims. Because we are a private-pay practice that provides superbills for out-of-network reimbursement, disclosures for payment purposes are typically initiated by you when you submit your superbill to your insurer.

3. Health Care Operations

We may use and disclose your PHI for our health care operations. These activities include, but are not limited to: quality assessment and improvement, clinical training and supervision, business planning, credentialing and licensing activities, compliance activities, and other administrative functions necessary to run our practice and ensure the quality and safety of care.

4. Uses and Disclosures That Require Your Written Authorization

Except as described in this Notice, we will not use or disclose your PHI without your written authorization. You may revoke any authorization in writing at any time, except to the extent that we have already taken action in reliance upon the authorization. The following uses and disclosures require your written authorization:

  • Psychotherapy notes. Under HIPAA, psychotherapy notes (sometimes called "process notes") receive special protection and require your specific written authorization before we may disclose them to any third party, with narrow exceptions for our own treatment, training, defense in legal proceedings, oversight by HHS, or as otherwise required by law. Psychotherapy notes are maintained separately from your general medical record.
  • Marketing purposes. We will not use or disclose your PHI for marketing purposes without your written authorization.
  • Sale of PHI. We will not sell your PHI without your written authorization.

5. Uses and Disclosures That Do Not Require Your Authorization

In certain limited situations, we may use or disclose your PHI without your authorization as permitted or required by law, including:

  • As required by law. We will disclose PHI when required to do so by applicable federal, state, or local law.
  • Public health activities. We may disclose PHI for public health purposes, such as reporting communicable diseases to public health authorities.
  • Victims of abuse, neglect, or domestic violence. We may disclose PHI to government authorities if we believe a patient is the victim of abuse, neglect, or domestic violence, as required or authorized by law.
  • Health oversight activities. We may disclose PHI to health oversight agencies for activities authorized by law, such as audits, investigations, inspections, and licensure actions.
  • Judicial and administrative proceedings. We may disclose PHI in response to a court order or, in some cases, in response to a subpoena or discovery request, subject to applicable legal protections.
  • Law enforcement purposes. We may disclose PHI to law enforcement officials for certain law enforcement purposes as required or permitted by law.
  • To avert a serious threat to health or safety. We may use and disclose PHI when necessary to prevent a serious and imminent threat to the health or safety of a person or the public, consistent with applicable law and ethical standards.
  • Workers' compensation. We may disclose PHI to the extent authorized by and necessary to comply with workers' compensation or similar programs.
  • Coroners, medical examiners, and funeral directors. We may disclose PHI to a coroner or medical examiner as necessary to carry out their duties.
  • Organ and tissue donation. We may disclose PHI to organizations involved in organ, eye, or tissue procurement, banking, or transplantation, if applicable.
  • Military and veterans' activities, national security, and intelligence. If you are a member of the armed forces, we may disclose PHI as required by military command authorities. We may also disclose PHI to authorized federal officials for intelligence, counterintelligence, and other national security activities.
  • Inmates. If you are an inmate of a correctional institution, we may disclose PHI to the institution or its agents as necessary for your health care, the health and safety of others, or the safety of the correctional institution.

6. Substance Use Disorder Records (42 CFR Part 2)

If you receive treatment for a substance use disorder from our practice, records relating to that treatment may be subject to additional federal confidentiality protections under 42 CFR Part 2. These records generally cannot be disclosed without your specific written consent, even to other health care providers, except in limited circumstances such as a medical emergency or as otherwise permitted by 42 CFR Part 2. A general HIPAA authorization is not sufficient to release substance use disorder treatment records.

7. Uses and Disclosures to Individuals Involved in Your Care

Unless you object, we may disclose PHI to a family member, close personal friend, or other person you identify as being involved in your care, or who helps pay for your care. In the event of your incapacity or an emergency, we will use our professional judgment to determine whether such a disclosure is in your best interest.

C. Your Rights Regarding Your PHI

1. Right to Inspect and Copy Your Records

You have the right to inspect and obtain a copy of your PHI contained in our designated record set. Your request must be in writing. We may charge a reasonable fee for copies as permitted by California law. We will respond to your request within 15 days (as required by California's Confidentiality of Medical Information Act) or 30 days (as permitted by HIPAA), whichever is sooner. In limited circumstances, we may deny your request, in whole or in part. If we deny your request, we will provide you with a written explanation and inform you of your right to have the denial reviewed.

Note regarding psychotherapy notes: You do not have a right under HIPAA to access psychotherapy notes. However, California law may provide you with a right to inspect psychotherapy notes under certain circumstances. We will comply with whichever law provides you with greater access.

2. Right to Amend Your Records

You have the right to request that we amend your PHI if you believe it is inaccurate or incomplete. Your request must be in writing and must include the reason for the amendment. We may deny your request in certain circumstances (for example, if the information was not created by us, is not part of your designated record set, or is accurate and complete). If we deny your request, we will provide you with a written explanation and inform you of your right to submit a statement of disagreement.

3. Right to an Accounting of Disclosures

You have the right to request an accounting of certain disclosures of your PHI made by us during the six years prior to your request (or a shorter period if you specify one). This accounting will not include disclosures made for treatment, payment, or health care operations, disclosures made to you or pursuant to your authorization, disclosures made for national security or law enforcement purposes, or certain other disclosures. Your request must be in writing. The first accounting in any 12-month period is free; we may charge a reasonable fee for subsequent requests.

4. Right to Request Restrictions

You have the right to request restrictions on certain uses and disclosures of your PHI for treatment, payment, or health care operations. We are not required to agree to your request, except in one circumstance: if you pay for a service entirely out of pocket (without submitting a claim to your insurer), you have the right to request that we not disclose PHI related to that service to your health plan, and we are required to honor that request. Because Altos Psychiatry is a private-pay practice, this right is particularly relevant — if you choose not to submit a superbill to your insurer, we will not disclose your treatment information to your health plan.

5. Right to Request Confidential Communications

You have the right to request that we communicate with you about health matters in a certain way or at a certain location. For example, you may ask that we contact you only by email, or that we send correspondence to a particular address. We will accommodate all reasonable requests.

6. Right to a Paper Copy of This Notice

You have the right to obtain a paper copy of this Notice at any time, even if you have previously received it or agreed to receive it electronically. You may request a paper copy from our office.

7. Right to Be Notified of a Breach

You have the right to be notified in the event of a breach of your unsecured PHI, as defined by HIPAA and the HITECH Act. We will provide notification in accordance with applicable federal and California law.

D. Changes to This Notice

We reserve the right to change this Notice at any time. Any changes will apply to PHI we already have about you as well as any information we receive in the future. We will make the revised Notice available at our office and on our website. You may request a copy of the current Notice at any time.

E. Complaints

If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with:

  • Our Practice: Altos Psychiatry, Inc. — see contact information below.
  • The U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Office for Civil Rights: hhs.gov/hipaa/filing-a-complaint or call 1-800-368-1019.

II. Your Rights Under California Law

California Confidentiality of Medical Information Act (CMIA) — Cal. Civ. Code §56 et seq.

In addition to your rights under federal HIPAA regulations, California law provides you with additional protections for your medical information. Where California law provides greater protection than HIPAA, we follow California law.

A. California Confidentiality of Medical Information Act (CMIA)

The CMIA generally prohibits health care providers from disclosing medical information without your prior written authorization, except in circumstances specifically permitted by law. Key provisions include:

  • Authorization required for most disclosures. We will obtain your written authorization before disclosing your medical information to third parties, except as permitted by law for treatment, payment, health care operations, or other specified purposes.
  • Sensitive conditions. California law provides heightened protections for information related to certain conditions, including mental health treatment, HIV/AIDS, genetic testing results, and substance use disorder treatment.
  • 15-day response requirement. Under the CMIA, we must respond to your request for access to your medical records within 15 days (shorter than the HIPAA 30-day timeframe).
  • Minor patients. California law establishes specific rules regarding the confidentiality of minors' medical information. Although our practice currently treats adults, we comply with all applicable provisions.
  • Employer access. Your employer generally does not have the right to access your medical information without your specific written authorization. We will not disclose your information to an employer or prospective employer without your consent, except as required by law.

B. California Mental Health Records Protections

Under California law (Welfare and Institutions Code §5328 et seq. and related statutes), information and records obtained in the course of providing mental health services are confidential and may not be disclosed except under specific circumstances, including:

  • With your informed written consent;
  • When there is a duty to warn of a serious threat of physical violence against a reasonably identifiable victim (California Civil Code §43.92, commonly known as the "Tarasoff duty");
  • When required by a court order;
  • When necessary to prevent a patient from harming themselves;
  • When required for reporting suspected child abuse, elder abuse, or dependent adult abuse;
  • To the extent necessary for a qualified peer review or quality assurance committee;
  • When otherwise specifically required or authorized by California or federal law.

C. Telehealth Privacy Protections

If you receive services via telehealth, the same privacy protections apply to your information as for in-person visits. In accordance with California Business and Professions Code §2290.5, we use HIPAA-compliant telehealth technology (SimplePractice) with end-to-end encryption. We do not record telehealth sessions. Your verbal consent to telehealth services is obtained prior to your first video visit and documented in your medical record.

Information submitted through website forms is retained for as long as necessary to process your inquiry and for a reasonable period thereafter for administrative and legal compliance purposes. IV. California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA) Notice

Effective Date: July 1, 2025

A. Applicability

The California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018, as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act of 2020 (collectively, "CCPA/CPRA"), provides California residents with specific rights regarding their personal information. The CCPA/CPRA applies to businesses that meet certain revenue or data-processing thresholds. It also contains important exemptions for health care providers.

HIPAA Exemption: To the extent that our collection, use, and disclosure of your information is governed by HIPAA — including all protected health information (PHI) and information collected, used, or disclosed in connection with your treatment, payment, or health care operations — that information is exempt from the CCPA/CPRA. The HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices above governs that information, not this section.

This CCPA/CPRA Notice applies to personal information we collect through our website that is not otherwise exempt under HIPAA — primarily the non-clinical information you provide through consultation request and referral forms before a patient relationship is established, and website analytics data.

B. Categories of Personal Information We Collect

In the preceding 12 months, we may have collected the following categories of personal information from California residents through our website:

  • Identifiers: Name, email address, phone number, IP address.
  • Internet or network activity: Browsing history on our Site, pages visited, interaction with our Site.
  • Professional or employment-related information: For referring providers — practice name, credentials, specialty.

C. How We Use This Information

We use non-exempt personal information for the business purposes described in Section III above (responding to inquiries, scheduling, improving our website, legal compliance). We do not use personal information for purposes materially different from those disclosed at the time of collection without providing you with notice.

D. Sale and Sharing of Personal Information

We do not sell your personal information. We have not sold personal information in the preceding 12 months. We do not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising purposes.

E. Your Rights Under the CCPA/CPRA

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights with respect to non-exempt personal information:

  • Right to Know. You may request that we disclose the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, the categories of sources from which we collected it, the business purposes for which we use it, and the categories of third parties with whom we share it.
  • Right to Delete. You may request that we delete personal information we have collected from you, subject to certain exceptions (e.g., legal compliance, completing a transaction).
  • Right to Correct. You may request that we correct inaccurate personal information we maintain about you.
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing. Because we do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising, this right is not currently applicable. If our practices change, we will provide a "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link on our Site.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination. We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your CCPA/CPRA rights. We will not deny you services, charge you different prices, or provide you with a different level of quality for exercising your rights.
  • Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information. To the extent we collect sensitive personal information outside the scope of HIPAA, you have the right to limit its use to purposes necessary to provide our services. In practice, we do not use sensitive personal information for purposes beyond what is necessary to respond to your inquiry.

F. How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of the rights described above, you may submit a verifiable consumer request by:

  • Emailing us at privacy@altospsychiatry.com
  • Calling us at (650) 555-0000

We will verify your identity before processing your request. You may also designate an authorized agent to submit a request on your behalf. We will respond to verifiable consumer requests within 45 days, as required by law.
