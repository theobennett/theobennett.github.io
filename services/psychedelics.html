Psychedelic-Assisted Therapy

A groundbreaking approach to healing profound emotional and psychological challenges.

What is Psychedelic-Assisted Therapy?

This is a groundbreaking form of therapy where a psychedelic substance (like psilocybin or ketamine) is administered in a safe, controlled, and medically-supervised environment. The experience is combined with psychotherapy to help you process emotions and gain deep insights that might be hard to access otherwise. It's designed to create profound and lasting change.

Who is it for?

This treatment is showing great promise for individuals with treatment-resistant depression, PTSD, and severe anxiety. It's for people looking for a fundamentally different approach to healing, particularly when other treatments have not been effective.

How does it work?

The process involves several stages: 1) Preparation sessions with a therapist to set goals and build trust. 2) The supervised psychedelic experience itself, which takes place in a comfortable, therapeutic setting. 3) Integration sessions afterward, where you and your therapist work to make sense of the experience and turn your insights into real-life changes. Safety and support are our top priorities throughout.
