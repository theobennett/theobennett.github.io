Ketamine & Psychedelic-Informed Therapy

FDA-approved ketamine-based treatments available now — with a trained eye toward the broader psychedelic horizon.

For patients with treatment-resistant depression, PTSD, and other conditions that haven't responded adequately to conventional approaches, ketamine-based therapies represent one of the most significant advances in psychiatry in decades. We offer these treatments today — carefully, within established regulatory frameworks, and as part of a comprehensive treatment plan.

We also hold deep expertise in the broader psychedelic-assisted therapy landscape. We view this as a potentially groundbreaking frontier for treating longstanding trauma and facilitating the formation of new neural pathways for healing. But groundbreaking doesn't mean rushing. We are waiting for regulation to mature and clinical trial data to solidify before offering modalities like psilocybin to our patients.

Current ketamine-based services

Two established, evidence-based treatment pathways for patients who haven't responded to conventional medication.

FDA-Approved

Spravato (Esketamine)

FDA-approved nasal spray for treatment-resistant depression (TRD) and major depressive disorder with acute suicidal ideation. Administered in-office under clinical supervision through a certified REMS program. Spravato is the only ketamine-based treatment currently billable to most insurance plans for TRD.

  • In-office administration with 2-hour monitoring period
  • REMS-certified practice and prescribing clinician
  • Insurance-billable for qualifying diagnoses
  • Structured treatment protocol with defined acute and maintenance phases
Schedule III

Ketamine (IM / Sublingual)

Intramuscular or sublingual ketamine for treatment-resistant depression and PTSD. Ketamine is a Schedule III medication that requires no special waiver beyond standard DEA registration. Strong evidence supports rapid reduction of depressive symptoms, particularly in patients who have not responded to multiple prior medication trials.

  • In-office intramuscular administration or supervised sublingual dosing
  • Rapid onset — many patients notice shifts within hours to days
  • Indicated for TRD and PTSD with inadequate prior response
  • Integrated with ongoing psychiatric care — not a standalone treatment

How we deliver these treatments

Ketamine-based therapies are powerful — and require clinical structure, not just a prescription.

Not everyone is a candidate. We conduct a comprehensive psychiatric and medical evaluation — including review of prior treatment trials, substance use history, cardiovascular health, and dissociative risk factors — before recommending ketamine-based treatment. This is a clinical decision, not a consumer one.

Before your first session, we walk through what to expect — physically, psychologically, and logistically. We set treatment goals, discuss the role ketamine plays within your broader care plan, and establish safety protocols. If you're working with a therapist, we coordinate with them to align preparation and integration work.

All ketamine-based treatments are administered in our office under direct clinical supervision. For Spravato, the REMS protocol requires a minimum 2-hour observation period with vital sign monitoring. For IM or sublingual ketamine, we provide a similar supervised environment. You will not drive yourself home from any session.

Ketamine can catalyze psychological shifts — but those shifts need clinical support to become lasting change. We schedule integration discussions after sessions, track outcomes with validated scales, and adjust the treatment protocol based on your response. Ketamine is a tool within a treatment plan, not the treatment plan itself.

Looking Forward

The broader psychedelic landscape

We believe psychedelic-assisted therapy has the potential to fundamentally change how psychiatry treats longstanding trauma, treatment-resistant depression, and other conditions where conventional approaches fall short. The evidence emerging from clinical trials — for psilocybin, MDMA, and other compounds — points to a mechanism of action that goes beyond symptom suppression: facilitating the formation of new neural pathways and enabling patients to process experiences that have been neurobiologically "stuck."

Our clinical team has training in psychedelic-assisted therapeutic frameworks, including preparation, dosing session facilitation, and integration. We are not newcomers to this space — we are clinicians who take it seriously enough to wait.

Our position: We are actively monitoring the regulatory landscape — including California SB 58 and successor legislation, FDA review processes, and emerging Phase III trial data. When legal pathways are established and clinical evidence meets our threshold of confidence, we intend to offer properly structured psilocybin-assisted and other psychedelic-assisted programs with full preparation and integration support. Until then, we believe the responsible path is patience.

Candidacy

Who ketamine-based treatment is for

Spravato and ketamine are not first-line treatments. They are most appropriate — and most effective — for patients who have already tried conventional approaches without adequate relief:

  • Treatment-resistant depression (inadequate response to two or more adequate medication trials)
  • Major depressive disorder with acute suicidal ideation or behavior (Spravato indication)
  • PTSD that has not responded adequately to first-line pharmacotherapy and psychotherapy
  • Severe depressive episodes where rapid symptom reduction is clinically warranted

Not appropriate for everyone. Ketamine-based treatments carry specific risks including dissociation, elevated blood pressure, and potential for misuse. Active substance use disorders, uncontrolled hypertension, and certain psychiatric conditions may be contraindications. Candidacy is determined through comprehensive clinical evaluation — not self-selection.
