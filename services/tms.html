TMS (Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation)

What is TMS?

TMS is a modern, non-invasive treatment that uses magnetic fields to gently stimulate specific areas of the brain that are underactive in people with depression. It’s like a targeted workout for your brain cells. The treatment is done while you are awake and comfortable, and it does not require anesthesia.

Who is it for?

TMS is an FDA-approved treatment primarily for adults with major depression who have not had good results from antidepressant medications. If you've tried different medications and still feel "stuck," TMS could be a great option.

How does it work?

You'll sit in a comfortable chair, and a small magnetic coil is placed gently on your head. The coil sends brief magnetic pulses to the target area. A typical course of treatment involves daily sessions, five days a week, for about 4-6 weeks. Each session lasts about 20-30 minutes, and you can drive yourself home or back to work immediately after.
