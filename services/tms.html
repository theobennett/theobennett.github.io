What is TMS?

Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS) is a non-invasive treatment that uses brief magnetic pulses to stimulate targeted areas of the brain — specifically the regions underactive in depression. It's performed while you're awake and seated, requires no anesthesia, and has no systemic side effects (unlike medications).

TMS is FDA-cleared for major depressive disorder and OCD, and has a substantial evidence base for treatment-resistant depression in particular.

Who is it for?

TMS is typically considered when a patient has not achieved adequate response from at least one or two adequate antidepressant trials. Most insurance payers require documentation of prior medication failures before approving TMS.

It's a particularly strong option for patients who are medication-intolerant, have concerns about systemic side effects, or are looking for a non-pharmacological component to their treatment.

Protocols we'll offer

Standard TMS

45-minute sessions, 5 days per week for 4–6 weeks. FDA-cleared for MDD and OCD. The most widely studied protocol.

SAINT Protocol (Accelerated TMS)

Stanford Accelerated Intelligent Neuromodulation Therapy — multiple shorter sessions per day over a compressed timeline. Shows rapid response potential for TRD. We intend to offer this alongside standard TMS.
