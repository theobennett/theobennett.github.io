Psychedelic-Assisted Therapy Future

We're tracking the evolving evidence and regulatory landscape for ketamine and psilocybin-assisted psychiatric treatment.

Not yet available. We intend to offer psychedelic-assisted treatment as legal frameworks in California mature and the evidence base continues to build. We'll announce availability through our patient newsletter and this page.

What the evidence shows

Ketamine and esketamine (Spravato) are currently the most established options for clinical use, with strong evidence for rapid reduction of depressive symptoms — particularly in treatment-resistant presentations. MDMA-assisted therapy for PTSD and psilocybin for treatment-resistant depression are in advanced clinical trials, with promising results.

We take a measured view. These are powerful treatments with real risks, and they require careful patient selection, preparation, and integration. We intend to offer them thoughtfully when the time is right — not opportunistically.

What we're planning

IM Ketamine Infusions

For treatment-resistant depression and PTSD. Ketamine is Schedule III and requires no waiver beyond standard DEA registration. We plan to offer this as an in-office service in Year 2.

Spravato (Esketamine)

FDA-approved nasal spray for TRD — the only ketamine-based treatment currently billable to insurance for TRD. Requires REMS certification for both the practice and prescribing clinician. We are planning this certification.

Psilocybin & MDMA (Future)

We are monitoring California SB 58 and successor legislation, as well as FDA review of MDMA-assisted therapy. When legal pathways open, we intend to offer properly structured programs with preparation and integration sessions.
