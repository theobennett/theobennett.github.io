Medication Management

Precision psychopharmacology — starting with a complete picture and adjusting until we get it right.

Overview

What it is

Psychiatric medication management is far more than writing a prescription. Done well, it's an ongoing clinical relationship: starting at the right medication and dose, monitoring for response and side effects, making adjustments informed by objective data, and managing the metabolic and physiological effects of long-term treatment.

We approach medication management as a continuous process — not a series of disconnected 15-minute appointments.

Our Approach

What makes our approach different

Three capabilities that distinguish precision psychopharmacology from standard prescribing.

Metabolic Monitoring

Many psychiatric medications cause weight gain, insulin resistance, and metabolic dysfunction — and most prescribers ignore it. Our founder holds board certification in Obesity Medicine. We actively monitor and manage these effects as part of routine care, reducing the side-effect burden that leads so many patients to stop effective medications.

Pharmacogenomic Testing

Where clinically appropriate, we use genetic testing to understand how your body metabolizes specific medications. This can significantly reduce the trial-and-error that characterizes much of psychiatric pharmacology — particularly for patients who've had unexpected side effects or unusual response patterns in the past.

Outcome Tracking

We use validated symptom scales (PHQ-9, GAD-7, ASRS, and others) at every visit to track progress objectively. This keeps treatment adjustments data-driven, not impressionistic — and gives both of us a clear picture of what's working and what isn't.

How it works

From your first evaluation to long-term maintenance, every phase of medication management is structured, transparent, and collaborative.

Your first visit is a 60–90 minute deep-dive into your psychiatric history, medical history, current symptoms, medication trials, family history, and treatment goals. We review past records, current labs, and build a diagnostic framework before making any medication decisions.

We explain the rationale behind every medication recommendation — mechanism of action, expected timeline, potential side effects, and how it fits into your overall treatment plan. If pharmacogenomic testing is appropriate, we order it at this stage to inform or refine our selection.

Follow-up visits are scheduled at intervals appropriate to your clinical needs — typically every 2–4 weeks during active adjustment, extending as you stabilize. At each visit, we administer validated symptom scales, review side effects, assess metabolic markers, and adjust doses or agents based on the data.

Once you've achieved stable remission, we transition to a maintenance cadence — typically every 1–3 months. We continue outcome tracking, metabolic monitoring, and periodic re-evaluation of whether your current regimen still represents the best path forward. Medication management is a relationship, not a transaction.

Candidacy

Who it's for

Medication management is appropriate for most psychiatric conditions including depression, anxiety, ADHD, bipolar disorder, PTSD, OCD, and psychotic disorders. It's particularly valuable if you've experienced:

  • Mixed or inadequate results with previous medications
  • Troublesome side effects — especially metabolic ones — that led you to stop treatment
  • Uncertainty about what you're taking and why
  • Multiple medication trials without a clear diagnostic framework
  • A desire for a more data-driven, transparent prescribing relationship
